The Problem

Algorithmic fatigue.

Streaming services are designed to keep you engaged, not to let you enjoy high-fidelity audio in peace. They compress your files, track your every click, and wrap your music in ads and algorithmic recommendations.

Void Player was built to do one thing perfectly: play local lossless audio files without a screen, without the cloud, and without compromises.

Hardware meets software.

A localized ecosystem designed for audiophiles and tinkerers.

▤

Tactile OLED Interface

Sometimes you just want to press a button. The physical unit features a fully programmed luma.oled interface for track skipping, volume control, ALSA Bluetooth device routing, and safe system reboots—without ever opening a terminal.

⇪

Drag & Drop Library

Forget standard SFTP clients. Upload .flac, .wav, and .mp3 files straight from your browser into the Pi's filesystem. Watch your storage capacity update in real-time.

◷

Granular Telemetry

A lightweight SQLite database tracks your top tracks, artists, and listening habits locally. No cloud tracking. Just pure, localized data visualization on the dashboard.

⎛

Headless Hardware Sync

The web dashboard isn't just a viewer—it's the nervous system. Adjust the Pi's default boot volume, configure OLED screen sleep timers to prevent burn-in, and dictate auto-play behavior directly from your browser over the local network.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I buy a Void Player?

No, this is a personal DIY hardware project. However, the architecture and design concepts are meant to inspire others to build their own local-first audio devices.

Why use VLC as the backend instead of MPD?

While Music Player Daemon (MPD) is the standard for headless Linux audio, the `python-vlc` bindings provided much faster prototyping for integrating custom SQLite telemetry triggers (like tracking exactly when a song is skipped vs. completed).

Can I output to an external USB DAC?

Yes. The ALSA routing system inside the Linux environment allows you to bypass the Pi's native audio and output pristine digital audio straight to an external DAC for audiophile-grade listening.

Is the source code available?

The code is currently private while under active development, but may be open-sourced on GitHub in the future with full wiring diagrams for the OLED and buttons.
